What is Email Verification?

Email verification is the process of validating whether an email address is authentic, active, and capable of receiving messages. The Email Verification API performs this validation in real time at the point of data capture, helping organizations ensure that email addresses provided by users are genuine and usable.

By filtering out disposable, inactive, or invalid email IDs, the API improves data quality, reduces communication failures, and mitigates fraud risks across digital onboarding and engagement workflows.

What the Email Verification API Checks

  • Format & Syntax Validity: Confirms whether the email follows standard formatting rules.
  • Domain Validity: Verifies that the domain exists and is properly configured to receive emails.
  • Mailbox Deliverability: Assesses whether the email address can successfully receive messages.
  • Disposable & Temporary Email Detection: Identifies one-time or fraudulent-use addresses.
  • Role-Based Email Identification: Flags generic addresses like info@, admin@, and support@.
  • Real-Time Integration: Integrate validation at point of capture for stronger data quality.

Interpreting Verification Results

  • Valid: Email is authentic, active, and deliverable. Safe for onboarding and communication.
  • Invalid: Email does not exist or cannot receive messages. Should be rejected.
  • Disposable / Temporary: Short-term address with higher fraud risk. Recommend blocking.
  • Inactive / Undeliverable: Mailbox unreachable or dormant. Impacts communication reliability.
  • Role-Based: Generic address not tied to an individual. May need extra identity checks.
